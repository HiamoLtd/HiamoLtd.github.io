Current Project - Wellington Fortifications

At this time, Hiamo is working to recreate the guns at a historic gun emplacement site in Wellington city, New Zealand. Many residents and tourists do not know about these sites and it is often hard to tell what these hulking concrete structures were used for.

We are helping to remedy this issue by bringing the historic armaments of these sites back to their original emplacements using Augmented Reality (AR) technology. Physical replicas are not an option at many of these sites, but using AR Hiamo is able to display these lost elements.

The current version of this application places a model of one of the guns on-site, allowing users to move around the replica and understand the scale of the object. Our work is now focussed on improving this gun model, enabling users to interact with it, and telling the story of both the gun and the site itself.

Future Plan

Hiamo aims to bring AR to numerous heritage sites around New Zealand and beyond. We want to work with site managers to bring an exciting interactive experience to historic landmarks. We aim to not only tell the stories of these places using our technology but also replicate lost parts of history and immerse visitors in the heritage around them. By bringing the objects at the sites to life, visitors can engage with the history which unfolded at the site and learn about the history in a new way.

Bridging Realities Outdoors

Our plan is to begin by focussing on developing virtual interactive objects and structures outdoors at historic locations. From there we want to explore the many other features we can implement into the application which will cater to a wide range of users. This could include:

  • Storytelling using an audio narration, background sound effects, or a virtual guide.

  • Using geolocation technology to redraw the landscape and create objects, structures, and even people on location as the user moves. This would allow users to explore their surroundings further while watching the virtual people and objects at work around them.

  • Time-lapse feature to allow users to view and control the change in the landscape over time.

Indoor Virtual Worlds

Hiamo is open to exploring the many ways to we can bring the life and energy of the outdoors into a smaller, controlled indoor environment. We can use our technology to create animated sculptures, virtual museums, and interactive table top environments. These can be utilised in transit areas, such as waiting rooms and boarding areas or used as interactive models of structures and objects such as building and floor plans. In doing so, this will allow users to learn and understand the material in a more impactful and tactile manner.
