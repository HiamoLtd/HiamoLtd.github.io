About Us

“Reconnecting explorers with historic landmarks through the lens of today’s innovation.”

Hiamo is bringing augmented reality (AR) to numerous heritage sites around New Zealand and beyond. We, at Hiamo, are currently bringing historic landmarks to life with interactive experiences and immersive storytelling of the events which unfolded at the site and the people who were involved. Our technology virtually recreates the lost and damaged parts of history to immerse visitors in the heritage around them so that they can better engage with the history and learn in a new way.

The Team

Hiamo is a young digital innovations company from Wellington, the creative capital of New Zealand. Led by CEO Alex Hockley and CTO Shaika Khan, and incubated from the Mahuki innovation accelerator program at Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa, Hiamo is developing creative, cutting edge technology for the New Zealand’s culture and heritage.




Hiamo was founded on the passion to reconnect Kiwi's to the land through historical events and stories which have shaped the landscape and evolved the communities.

The Market

5731 listed heritage sites in New Zealand.

Over 227,000 visits to staffed New Zealand heritage sites annually.

Over $16 million annual revenue from New Zealand heritage sites.

Over $24 billion annual revenue from UNESCO world heritage sites worldwide.

Hiamo works with the managers of heritage sites around the country to bring exciting interactive experience to historic landmarks, bringing them to life by allowing visitors to engage with history in a new way.
Many historic site managers need to invest heavily in reproductions to engage visitors. Hiamo offers a sustainable and stimulating alternative by using augmented reality to virtually reproduce the lost or damaged objects on site. This approach is both cost-effective to build while also being easier to update and extend.
Our solution goes beyond restoration and encourages explorative learning for all ages using cutting-edge technology. The opportunity to interact with the past in this way also offers an avenue for lifelong learning among our users and displays history in a new and exciting light.

Projects


There are many projects Hiamo is keen to work on. Hiamo believes that it is important to utilise the convenience of technology available at your fingertips such as AR mobile apps. The ability to merge together the virtual and real word in real time can be beneficial to many domains.

Current Projects

Hiamo is currently working with to bring to life the many coastal fortifications and anti-aircraft batteries around NZ. Working with military heritage sites is one progression, enabling us to bring the technology to more of New Zealand’s coastal defence sites. The extensive array of anti-aircraft batteries around the country are one form of site that allow us to develop for numerous sites as once. Other fortifications are also strong candidates for this technology, such as the North Head defences in Auckland, Matiu (Somes Island) in Wellington, and others in Christchurch and Dunedin.

Future Plans

There are many projects Hiamo aims to work on in the future. Our technology can be utilised in many areas both within and separate to Heritage sites.

Bridging Realities Outdoors

Hiamo’s plan is to begin by focussing on initial interactions and engagement with the historic objects at heritage sites. Heritage sites, in particular, harbour many lost or forgotten historical objects or structures and stories to be told. Hiamo aims to expand beyond these fortification sites as well. Looking specifically and New Zealand’s pre-European history, Maori defence works and villages such as Pa, are also areas we would be excited to work with. Other heritage sites such as historic buildings, early European settlement sites, flora and fauna, and remnants of New Zealand’s industrial history such as logging and mining, are also strong candidates.

We want to explore the many other features we can implement into the application which will cater to a wide range of users. This could include:

  • Story telling using an audio narration, background sound effects, or a virtual guide.
  • Using geolocation technologies to provide users an interactive and evolving storytelling experience as they walk around a heritage site.
  • Time-lapse feature to allow users to view the change in a landscape over time.

Indoor Virtual Worlds

Hiamo are open to exploring the many ways to we can bring the life and energy of the outdoors into a smaller, controlled indoor environment. We can use our technology to create animated sculptures, virtual museums, and interactive table top environments. These can be utilised in transit areas, such as waiting rooms and boarding areas, or used as interactive models of structures and objects such as building and floor plans. In doing so, this will allow users to learn and understand material in a more impactful and tactile manner.
