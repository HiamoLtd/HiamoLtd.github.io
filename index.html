About Us

Hiamo is working to bring AR to numerous heritage sites around New Zealand and beyond. We want to work with site managers to bring an exciting interactive experience to historic landmarks.

We aim to not only tell the stories of these places using our technology, but also replicate lost parts of history and immerse visitors in the heritage around them. By bringing the objects at the sites to life, visitors can engage with history which unfolded at the site and learn about the history in a new way.

Hiamo Meaning

Hiamo is a te reo Māori word which has many meanings, depending on the context it is used in. The Te Aka Online Māori Dictionary lists some definitions as:

1. (verb) to be thrilled, excited, exciting.
2. (verb) to be elevated, exalted, impressed.

Current Project - Wellington Fortifications

At this time, Hiamo is working to recreate the guns at a historic gun emplacement site in Wellington city, New Zealand. Many residents and tourists do not know about these sites and it is often hard to tell what these hulking concrete structures were used for.

We are helping to remedy this issue by bringing the historic armaments of these sites back to their original emplacements using Augmented Reality (AR) technology. Physical replicas are not an option at many of these sites, but using AR Hiamo is able to display these lost elements.

The current version of this application places a model of one of the guns on-site, allowing users to move around the replica and understand the scale of the object. Our work is now focussed on improving this gun model, enabling users to interact with it, and telling the story of both the gun and the site itself.

For more information on our current and future work, visit our projects page.
